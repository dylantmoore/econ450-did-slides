Another Case Study: Drones in Pakistan

Mir & Moore (International Studies Quarterly, 2019)

Research Question: Did the US drone program reduce insurgent violence in Pakistan?

Context: The US drone program began in January 2008 in North Waziristan, part of Pakistan's Federally Administered Tribal Areas (FATA).

This was a sustained campaign of targeted killings aimed at disrupting militant networks operating in the region.

Why this matters: Counterterrorism policy debates often hinge on whether such programs "work"—but what does the evidence actually show?
