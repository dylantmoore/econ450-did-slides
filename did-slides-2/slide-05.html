When Quantitative Methods Aren't Enough

"Our work does not 'prove drones work', or support any similarly simplistic statements."

— Mir & Moore

The analysis rests heavily on qualitative evidence:

  • 66 interviews with officials, journalists, and former Taliban members
  • The quantitative analysis shows patterns
  • The qualitative evidence makes them meaningful

Key insight: When the identifying assumptions of your research design are imperfect, supplementary evidence becomes essential—not optional.

This is a case where mixed methods were not just a nice addition—they were necessary to make credible claims about causality.
