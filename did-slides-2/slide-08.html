Why Pre-Trends Can Be Misleading Here

Discussion: Is DID appropriate for this setting?

The Core Problem: A Regime Change

Before the layoff, everyone still had a job. The world before layoffs tells us nothing about who will struggle to find new work after the layoff.

Think about it: TAA is given to workers whose layoffs were caused by trade—imports, offshoring. Program administrators are trying to help workers who they believe will have a harder time finding new employment.

The selection story: If TAA goes to workers expected to have worse trajectories post-layoff, then comparing approved to denied workers — even with parallel pre-trends — would understate the true benefit of the program.

Key insight: Parallel trends in the pre-layoff period doesn't imply parallel counterfactual trends in the post-layoff period. The layoff itself is a regime change that reshuffles who's advantaged.
