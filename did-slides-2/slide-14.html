Audits Have Long-Run Deterrence Effects

Key Finding from NRP Random Audits

Audited taxpayers pay ~$300 more per year for 14+ years after the audit

3.2x

Deterrence multiplier

Present value of future compliance

= 3.2 x initial audit revenue

Why the RCT matters here: You cannot estimate deterrence from non-random audits! If the IRS targets people who would have changed behavior anyway, you'd conflate selection with deterrence.

This is "individual deterrence"—the effect on the person who was audited. There may also be "general deterrence" effects on others who hear about audits.
