Building Confidence in Your Results

Remember: “no causes in, no causes out.” All causal inference combines assumptions and data. Here are types of evidence that can support your assumptions.

Placebo Tests

Apply the DID method where you know the effect should be zero. If you get zero, your method seems to work in this context.

Context & Qualitative Evidence

Tell a story about why you believe the two groups would have evolved similarly. Institutional details matter.

Bouncing Post-Treatment Effects

If effects flip between positive and negative after treatment—even significant ones—be suspicious unless you have a good explanation.

Change in Trajectory

Even imperfect pre-trends might be okay if there is a clear break at the moment of treatment.
