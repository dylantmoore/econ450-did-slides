DID as a Regression

$$Y_{it} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \color{#009E73}{\textit{Treat}_{i}} + \beta_2 \cdot \color{#D55E00}{\textit{Post}_{t}} + \color{#0072B2}{\boldsymbol{\beta_3}} \cdot (\color{#009E73}{\textit{Treat}_{i}} \times \color{#D55E00}{\textit{Post}_{t}}) + u_{it}$$

\(\beta_0\) = baseline mean (control group, pre-period)

\(\beta_1\) = time-invariant difference between groups

\(\beta_2\) = common time trend (change for the control group)

\(\boldsymbol{\beta_3}\) = the DID estimate — the causal effect of treatment!
