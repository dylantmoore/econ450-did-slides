“No Causes In, No Causes Out”

— Nancy Cartwright — but this is a logical fact, not merely her opinion

This is not a philosophical opinion. It is a logical impossibility to derive causal conclusions from data alone—just as you cannot derive an “ought” from an “is.” Even RCTs require assumptions (proper randomization, no attrition, compliance). The assumptions may be easy to believe, but they still exist.

In DID, the key assumption is parallel trends—a statement about an unobservable counterfactual that no amount of data can directly verify.
