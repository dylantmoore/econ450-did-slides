From DID to Event Studies

Standard DID gives us one number: the average treatment effect across all post-periods.

But what if…

  • The effect takes time to appear?
  • The effect grows or fades over time?
  • We want to test parallel trends more rigorously?

Event studies solve this by estimating a separate effect for each time period relative to treatment.

Event Study = A series of DIDs, one for each time period

Lecture Notes: The Simplest Way to Think About Event Studies

Here is probably the easiest way to understand what an event study plot shows:

  1. Plot the outcome variable for the treatment group over time.
  2. Plot the outcome variable for the control group over time on the same graph.
  3. Slide one line up or down so that the two lines overlap at one point in time (the reference period, usually t = −1).
  4. The gap between the two lines at every other point in time is what the event study is plotting.

That is it. The event study coefficients are just the difference between the two groups' outcomes after forcing them to overlap at the reference period.

The pre-treatment coefficients tell you whether the two groups were on similar trajectories before the policy. The post-treatment coefficients tell you how the groups diverged after the policy—and those divergences are your estimates of the causal effect (if you trust the parallel trends assumption).
